The Quiet Joy of Watching Things Disappear

In the late afternoon, when the light goes amber and the windows turn into mirrors, I find a strange comfort in losing things on purpose.

The smallest pleasures are often borrowed — a passing word, a half-heard joke, a pebble pocketed and forgotten by Tuesday. They never really belong to us, and that's the point.

A friend once told me that good things, given freely, leave a sweeter trace than things kept tightly. I think she was right, though I can't remember exactly what she had given me at the time.

There is a kind of theater to letting go. The stage lights dim, the curtain shivers, and somewhere offstage a small creature with a burlap sack is already running.
